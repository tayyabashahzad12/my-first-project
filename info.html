HISTORY OF NEANDERTHAL

  1. The Neanderthals have a long evolutionary history.
  2. The earliest known examples of Neanderthal-like fossils are around 430,000 years old.
  3. The best-known Neanderthals lived between about 130,000 and 40,000 years ago, after which all physical evidence of them vanishes.
  4. Neanderthals are an extinct species of hominids that were the closest relatives to modern human beings.
  5. They lived throughout Europe and parts of Asia from about 400,000 until about 40,000 years ago, and they were adept at hunting large Ice Age animals.
  1. Neanderthals are genetically distinct from modern humans, but are more closely related to us than chimpanzees are.
  2. The Neanderthal and modern human lineages diverged about 550,000 years ago.
  3. So far, we have no evidence of Neanderthal mtDNA lineages in modern humans.
